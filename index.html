My Story

I think I have a pretty unique personal history. I grew up in one of the biggest cities in China, and when I was 14, I enrolled in an all-boy boarding school in Virginia. The transition from a bustling city to a small school in the mountains was challenging, yet it shaped my present identity.

I learned to be adaptable, to navigate diverse settings. The experience made me independent, more self-sufficient. Living in a communal enviroment with people who came from different countries, communities, family backgrounds eriched my abilities for teamwork and collaboration.

Now that I moved again to LA, I found myself appreciating the convinience of cities more than when I was a kid, but I sometimes miss the tranquil life in small towns. The fusion of urban and rural life styles and the exposure to a global community led me to create my own identity.
